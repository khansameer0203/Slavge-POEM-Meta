Research Protocol

Updated Systematic Review and Meta-analysis of the Efficacy and Safety of Peroral Endoscopic Myotomy in Achalasia Patients with Failed Previous Interventions

Background

Achalasia is a rare primary esophageal motility disorder characterized by impaired relaxation of the lower esophageal sphincter and absent esophageal peristalsis. While treatment modalities such as pneumatic dilation, laparoscopic Heller myotomy, botulinum toxin injection, and prior peroral endoscopic myotomy (POEM) provide symptomatic relief, treatment failure remains a significant clinical challenge, necessitating salvage interventions.

Peroral endoscopic myotomy has emerged as an effective minimally invasive option for patients with persistent or recurrent symptoms following previous interventions. In 2021, Tan et al. published the first comprehensive systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating the efficacy and safety of POEM in patients with failed prior treatment. Their review included studies published through 2019 and demonstrated high technical and clinical success with acceptable adverse event rates.

Since then, numerous additional studies with larger cohorts and longer follow-up periods have been published. Preliminary literature screening has identified at least fifteen eligible studies published after 2019, representing approximately a 36% increase in cumulative sample size compared with the previous review. These newer studies may substantially improve the precision of pooled estimates and provide updated evidence regarding clinical efficacy, durability, adverse events, and reflux outcomes.

An updated systematic review and meta-analysis is therefore warranted.

PICO Framework

Population

Patients with achalasia who previously underwent any one or more failed therapeutic interventions.

Intervention

Salvage Peroral endoscopic myotomy (POEM).

Comparator

Where available: Treatment-naïve patients, alternative salvage procedures, or different prior intervention groups. (Studies without comparators are also eligible).

Outcomes

Primary: Technical success, Clinical success.
Secondary: Decided based on reporting frequency in screened literature.

Eligibility Criteria

Inclusion Criteria

  • Adult patients with achalasia
  • Previous failed intervention before POEM
  • Original clinical studies
  • Prospective or retrospective cohort studies
  • Randomized trials
  • Studies reporting at least one outcome of interest

Exclusion Criteria

  • Case reports
  • Case series with fewer than 10 patients
  • Reviews, editorials, and letters
  • Conference abstracts without sufficient data
  • Animal studies
  • Pediatric-only populations

Information Sources & Search Strategy

Databases to be Searched

  • PubMed/MEDLINE
  • Embase
  • Scopus
  • Web of Science
  • Cochrane Library
  • Reference lists of included studies and previous systematic reviews

Search Terms

Searches will combine controlled vocabulary and free-text terms related to: Achalasia, Peroral endoscopic myotomy, POEM, Failed treatment, Recurrent achalasia, Salvage therapy, Redo POEM, Prior intervention. No language restrictions will be applied where feasible.

Study Selection & Data Extraction

Study Selection Process

The review process will be reported according to the PRISMA 2020 guidelines. Two independent reviewers will screen titles and abstracts, followed by full-text assessment. Disagreements will be resolved through consensus or consultation with a third reviewer. Invite links to the screening software have been distributed to the relevant authors.

Data Extraction Fields

Data extracted will include:

  • Study characteristics: Country, design, enrollment period, sample size, mean age, sex, achalasia subtype, previous intervention type, and time since previous intervention.
  • Procedural & clinical outcomes: POEM procedural characteristics, follow-up duration, clinical outcomes, and safety outcomes.

Risk of Bias Assessment

  • The methodological quality of observational studies will be assessed using the Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (NOS).
  • Randomized studies, if identified, will be evaluated using the Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) tool.

Statistical Analysis

Meta-analysis will be conducted using R (meta/metafor packages) or Comprehensive Meta-Analysis software.

  • Dichotomous outcomes: Pooled proportions with 95% confidence intervals (CI) will be calculated using a random-effects model.
  • Continuous outcomes: Synthesized using pooled mean differences or standardized mean differences where appropriate.
  • Heterogeneity: Assessed using Cochran’s Q test and the I² statistic.
  • Publication bias: Evaluated using funnel plots and Egger’s regression test when sufficient studies (≥10) are available.
  • Sensitivity analyses: Performed by excluding studies with high risk of bias and by leave-one-out analysis.

Subgroup Analyses

Subgroup analyses will be considered according to the type of previous intervention, geographic region, study design, follow-up duration, and achalasia subtype.
